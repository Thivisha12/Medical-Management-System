First Aid for Heart Attack

  • Call emergency services immediately (e.g., 911 in the US).
  • Help the person sit or lie down in a comfortable position.
  • Loosen tight clothing, especially around the neck and chest.
  • Stay with the person until medical help arrives.

First Aid for Fainting

  • Lay the person flat and raise their legs.
  • Loosen tight clothing around the neck.
  • Apply a cool cloth to the forehead.

Stop Bleeding

  • Apply direct pressure to a wound using a clean cloth or bandage to stop bleeding.
  • Elevate the injured area if possible.

Treating Burns

  • Cool the affected area under cold running water for at least 10 minutes.
  • Cover the burn with a clean, non-adhesive dressing.
  • Seek medical attention for severe burns.

First Aid for Insect Bites

  • Remove the stinger if present.
  • Clean the area with soap and water.
  • Apply a cold pack to reduce swelling.

First Aid for Cuts and Scrapes

  • Clean the wound with soap and water.
  • Apply pressure to stop bleeding.
  • Cover the wound with a sterile bandage.

First Aid for Sprains

  • Rest, ice, compress, and elevate the injured area (RICE).
  • Apply an elastic bandage to reduce swelling.

Managing Allergic Reactions

  • If someone experiences a severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis), administer an epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen) if available.
  • Seek emergency medical help immediately.

Recognizing Stroke Symptoms

  • Remember the FAST acronym - Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call emergency services.
  • Act quickly if you suspect someone is having a stroke.

Heat-related Illnesses

  • Keep hydrated and stay cool during hot weather to prevent heat exhaustion or heatstroke.
  • Move to a shaded area, rest, and drink water if you or someone else shows signs of heat-related illness.
